Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

1. What is the purpose of this application?

This application is designed to allow aspiring photographers and organizers to participate in and manage online photo contests. It includes features for organizing contests, submitting photos, and participating as jury members.

2. Who can use this application?

The app serves two main user groups:

  • Organizers: Users who create and manage photo contests.
  • Photo Junkies: Photographers who can register, participate in contests, and, with a certain ranking, be invited to serve as jurors.

3. Do I need to register to use the app?

Public Area: No registration is needed to view public content such as the landing page with the latest winning photos.

Private Area: Yes, registration is required to participate in contests and access the dashboard.

4. How do I register as a Photo Junkie?

You can register by filling out the registration form, which requires a username, first name, last name, and password.

5. What features are available for Photo Junkies?

Photo Junkies can:

  • View and participate in open contests.
  • Upload photos for contests.
  • Track participation in ongoing and finished contests.
  • View scores, feedback, and rankings once a contest ends.

6. What features are available for Organizers?

Organizers can:

  • Create and manage contests.
  • Invite jurors to judge contests.
  • Review and score photo submissions.
  • Announce winners and distribute awards.

7. How are contests structured?

Contests are divided into three phases:

  • Submission Phase: Participants submit their photos.
  • Judging Phase: Jurors review and score the submissions.
  • Results Phase: Winners are announced and awards are distributed.

8. Can I edit my photo submission?

No, once you submit your photo along with its title and story, these cannot be edited. Make sure you are satisfied with your submission before finalizing it.

9. How is the jury process conducted?

Jurors score photos from 1-10 and leave comments. If a photo does not match the contest category, jurors can flag it, automatically assigning a score of 0. A default score of 3 is awarded if a photo is not reviewed by a juror.

10. How are participants ranked?

Participants accumulate points through contest participation:

  • Winning a contest: 50 points
  • Second place: 30 points
  • Third place: 20 points
  • Participation: 10 points

11. How does the point system work?

Points are awarded as follows:

  • Winning a contest: 50 points
  • Second place: 30 points
  • Third place: 20 points
  • Participation: 10 points

12. What are the different ranking levels?

  • Beginner: 0-100 points
  • Intermediate: 101-500 points
  • Advanced: 501-1000 points
  • Expert: 1001-5000 points
  • Master: 5001+ points

13. How can I create a new contest as an organizer?

To create a contest, you will need to provide:

  • Contest title
  • Contest description
  • Submission deadline
  • Judging criteria

14. Can I choose a cover photo for my contest?

Yes, organizers can upload a cover photo, use an existing URL, or select a photo from previously uploaded images.

15. What happens when a contest ends?

Once a contest is finished, participants can:

  • View their scores and feedback
  • See the winning photos
  • Receive their awards

16. How are ties handled in contest results?

If multiple participants tie for the top spots, the positions and points are shared. For example, two participants tied for 1st place would each receive 40 points, while other positions adjust accordingly.

17. Can I be a jury member?

Only users with the ranking of "Master" or higher can be invited to serve as a jury member. Organizers automatically have the ability to serve as jurors.

18. How are the contests categorized?

Each contest belongs to a specific category (e.g., landscape, portrait, abstract), which is visible throughout the contest and must be adhered to by participants.

19. Is there a way to view past contests I participated in?

Yes, Photo Junkies can view both ongoing contests and finished contests they participated in. They will be able to see their scores and reviews once the contest has ended.

20. How long does each phase of a contest last?

Organizers can set the duration for each phase:

  • Submission Phase: 1-2 weeks
  • Judging Phase: 1 week
  • Results Phase: 1-2 days

21. How can I contact support for issues or feedback?

For any issues or feedback, please reach out to our support team through the contact form available on the website.
